ECO-Code

ECO-Code (Encyclopedia of Chess Openings) is the term for an internationally used system for systematization of chess openings. The chess variations will be presented and evaluated with symbols. It doesn't use words, so that the presentation is internationally understandable.

Openings covered

Volume A: Flank openings

  • Benkö Gambit
  • Benoni Defence
  • Bird's Opening
  • Dutch Defence
  • English Opening
  • Irregular openings, etc.
  • Old Indian Defence
  • Réti Opening

Volume B: Semi-Open Games other than the French Defence

  • Alekhine's Defence
  • Caro-Kann Defence
  • Modern Defence
  • Pirc Defence
  • Scandinavian Defence, etc.
  • Sicilian Defence

Volume C: Open Games and the French Defence

  • Four Knights Game, etc.
  • French Defence
  • Italian (Giuoco Piano and Two Knights)
  • King's Gambit
  • Petrov's Defence
  • Philidor Defence
  • Ruy Lopez
  • Scotch Game

Volume D: Closed Games and Semi-Closed Games (including Grünfeld Defence but not other Indian Defences)

  • Queen's Gambit
    • Accepted
    • Declined (Slav, Orthodox, Tarrasch, Tartakower, Albin Countergambit, etc.)
  • Grünfeld Defence
  • Queen's Pawn Game, etc.

Volume E: Indian Defences (other than Grünfeld Defence and Old Indian Defence)

  • Nimzo-Indian Defence
  • Queen's Indian Defence
  • King's Indian Defence

Main ECO codes

Volume A

  • White first moves other than 1.e4, 1.d4 (A00‑A39)
  • 1.d4 without 1...d5, 1...♘f6: Atypical replies to 1.d4 (A40‑A44)
  • 1.d4 ♘f6 without 2.c4: Atypical replies to 1...♘f6 (A45‑A49)
  • 1.d4 ♘f6 2.c4 without 2...e6, 2...g6: Atypical Indian systems (A50‑A79)
  • 1.d4 f5: Dutch Defence (A80-A99)

Volume B

  • 1.e4 without 1...c5, 1...e6, 1...e5 (B00‑B09)
  • 1.e4 c6: Caro-Kann Defence (B10‑B19)
  • 1.e4 c5: Sicilian Defence (B20‑B99)

Volume C

  • 1.e4 e6: French Defence (C00‑C19)
  • 1.e4 e5: Double King Pawn games (C20‑C99)

Volume D

  • 1.d4 d5: Double Queen Pawn games (D00‑D69)
  • 1.d4 ♘f6 2.c4 g6 with 3...d5: Grünfeld Defence (D70‑D99)

Volume E

  • 1.d4 ♘f6 2.c4 e6: Indian systems with ...e6 (E00‑E59)
  • 1.d4 ♘f6 2.c4 g6 without 3...d5: Indian systems with ...g6 (except Grünfeld) (E60‑E99)
