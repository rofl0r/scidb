TITLE ECO-Code

<!-- Taken from http://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/ECO-Code -->

<p>
ECO-Code (Encyclopedia of Chess Openings) is the term for an internationally used
system for systematization of chess openings. The chess variations will be presented
and evaluated with symbols. It doesn't use words, so that the presentation is
internationally understandable.
</p>

  <li><a href="#Main">Main ECO codes</a></li>

</ul>

<h2 id="Openings">Openings covered</h2>

<box>
  <p>
    Volume <b>A</b>: Flank openings
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Benkö Gambit</li>
    <li>Benoni Defence</li>
    <li>Bird's Opening</li>
    <li>Dutch Defence</li>
    <li>English Opening</li>
    <li>Irregular openings, etc.</li>
    <li>Old Indian Defence</li>
    <li>Réti Opening</li>
  </ul>
</box>

<box>
  <p>
    Volume <b>B</b>: Semi-Open Games other than the French Defence
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Alekhine's Defence</li>
    <li>Caro-Kann Defence</li>
    <li>Modern Defence</li>
    <li>Pirc Defence</li>
    <li>Scandinavian Defence, etc.</li>
    <li>Sicilian Defence</li>
  </ul>
</box>

<box>
  <p>
    Volume <b>C</b>: Open Games and the French Defence
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Four Knights Game, etc.</li>
    <li>French Defence</li>
    <li>Italian (Giuoco Piano and Two Knights)</li>
    <li>King's Gambit</li>
    <li>Petrov's Defence</li>
    <li>Philidor Defence</li>
    <li>Ruy Lopez</li>
    <li>Scotch Game</li>
  </ul>
</box>

<box>
  <p>
    Volume <b>D</b>: Closed Games and Semi-Closed Games
      (including Grünfeld Defence but not other Indian Defences)
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Queen's Gambit
      <ul type="square">
        <li>Accepted</li>
        <li>Declined (Slav, Orthodox, Tarrasch, Tartakower, Albin Countergambit, etc.)</li>
      </ul>
    </li>
    <li>Grünfeld Defence</li>
    <li>Queen's Pawn Game, etc.</li>
  </ul>
</box>

<box>
  <p>
    Volume <b>E</b>: Indian Defences (other than Grünfeld Defence and Old Indian Defence)
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li>Nimzo-Indian Defence</li>
    <li>Queen's Indian Defence</li>
    <li>King's Indian Defence</li>
  </ul>
</box>
