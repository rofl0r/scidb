INDEX Range specifiers (CQL)
TITLE CQL - Range specifiers

<p>
Many keyword options are followed by optional range specifiers. A range
specifier consists of either a single integer or two integers separated
by whitespace.
</p>

<p>
If a range specifier has one integer, it denotes that integer. Otherwise
it denotes all the integers between its first and second integers inclusive.
</p>

<p>
A special meaning has the value <i>unlimited</i>. If <i>unlimited</i> is a
single argument, the range is completely unlimited, this means all
values are included. If the lower range is <i>unlimited</i>, the meaning
is negative unlimited. If the upper range is <i>unlimited</i>, the meaning is
positive unlimited.
</p>

<note>
The special range <i>unlimited</i> is not part of the CQL standard.
</note>

<h3>Examples</h3>

<p>
The match list can have a <cql>:year</cql> keyword denoting the range of
years in which the game in question must lie in order for a match to occur.
</p>

<verb>
<cql>match:year</cql> 1934
</verb>

<p>
Denotes the year 1934. <code>1934</code> is the range specifier.
</p>

<verb>
<cql>match:year</cql> 1934 2000
</verb>

<p>
Denotes the years between 1934 and 2000 inclusive. The range specifier is
<code>1934</code> <code>2000</code>.
</p>

<verb>
<cql>match:year</cql> 1934 unlimited
</verb>

<p>
Denotes all the years after 1933.
</p>

<verb>
<cql>match:year</cql> unlimited 1934
</verb>

<p>
Denotes all the years before 1935.
</p>

<verb>
<cql>match:elo</cql> unlimited
</verb>

<p>
Match all games where one player has an ELO rating.
</p>
