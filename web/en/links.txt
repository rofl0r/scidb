  <div class="header">
  Chess games
  </div>

  <p>
  The most games are available in
  <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portable_Game_Notation">PGN Format<link/></a>.
  This format can be viewed or imported in Scidb.
  </p>

  <p>
  Mark Crowther's <a target="_blank" href="http://www.theweekinchess.com">The Week in Chess<link/></a>
  is providing daily chess news and games. Furthermore this site provides
  <a target="_blank" href="http://theweekinchess.com/twic">chess archives<link/></a> with high quality.
  </p>

  <p>
  The site <a target="_blank" href="http://www.nowinchess.com/games.php">Now In Chess<link/></a> by
  Gerardo Fernandez is tracking games of high quality not contained in TWIC
  archives. But it seems that this page is abondened.
  </p>

  <p>
  <a target="_blank" href="http://ficsgames.org">FICS<link/></a> is providing a huge archive
  with Internet games of different variants:
  Normal chess, Chess 960 (wild/fr), Giveaway Chess (wild/26), Losers Chess (wild/17),
  Three-check Chess (wild/25), Bughouse (wild/24), and Crazyhouse (wild/23).
  </p>

  <p>
  "PGN Mentor" provides PGN archives of
  <a target="_blank" href="http://www.pgnmentor.com/files.html#players">individual players<link/></a>
  and
  <a target="_blank" href="http://www.pgnmentor.com/files.html#events">different tournaments<link/></a>.
  </p>

  <p>
  <a target="_blank" href="http://www.365chess.com/">365Chess.com</a> provides an opening explorer and allows to search for games in the database.
  </p>

  <div class="header">
  Chess engines
  </div>

  <p>
  Many chess engines are available. A good listing is provided by
  <a target="_blank" href="http://www.computerchess.org.uk/ccrl/4040">CCRL 40/40<link/></a>,
  which allows an overview over the strength of the engines.
  </p>

  <p>
  Of particular significance are:
  </p>

  <dir>
    <li>
    <a target="_blank" href="http://stockfishchess.org">Stockfish<link/></a>:
    This engine is pre-installed in Scidb.
    Scidb's version is providing an unique feature, the support of variant
    <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-check_chess">Three-Check Chess<link/></a>.
    </li>

    <li>
    <a target="_blank" href="http://www.vlasak.biz/critter">Critter<link/></a>:
    Also a really strong engine, unfortunately not open source.
    </li>

    <li>
    <a target="_blank" href="http://sjeng.org/indexold.html">Sjeng<link/></a>:
    This engine - also pre-installed in Scidb - is supporting the chess variants
    <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crazyhouse">Crazyhouse<link/></a> and
    <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antichess">Antichess<link/></a>
    (<a target="_blank" href="http://suicidechess.ca/index.php">Suicide<link/></a>,
    <a target="_blank" href="http://www6.chessclub.com/help/Giveaway">Giveaway<link/></a>,
    <!-- <a target="_blank" href="http://loserschess.org/">Losers<link/></a>). HACKED -->
    <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Losing_chess">Losers<link/></a>).
    </li>

    <li>
    <a href="http://firenzina.wikispaces.com/">Firenzina<link/></a>:
    Also a remarkable chess engine.
    </li>
  </dir>

  <div class="header">
  Other chess databases
  </div>

  <p>
  <a target="_blank" href="http://scid.sourceforge.net">Scid<link/></a> is probably the best known free
  chess database, originally developed by Shane Hudson. The today's version
  of Scid is rather buggy.
  </p>

  <p>
  <a target="_blank" href="scidvspc.sourceforge.net">Scid vs. PC<link/></a>, maintained by
  Steven Atkinson, is a bug-fixed version of Scid, with focus on
  greater usability.
  </p>

  <p>
  Gerhard Kalab's
  <a target="_blank" href="http://code.google.com/p/scidonthego/">Scid on the Go<link/></a>
  lets you use Scid for an Android tablet.
  </p>

  <p>
  <a target="_blank" href="http://chessx.sourceforge.net">ChessX<link/></a>, currently developed
  by Jens Nissen, provides a free chess database specialized on the
  <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portable_Game_Notation">PGN Format<link/></a>.
  </p>

  <p>
  No longer in development, but still of interest, is the Java application
  <a target="_blank" href="http://jose-chess.sourceforge.net">José<link/></a>.
  </p>
