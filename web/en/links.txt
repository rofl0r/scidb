  <div class="header">
  Chess games
  </div>

  <p>
  The most games are available in
  <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portable_Game_Notation">PGN Format</a>.
  This format can be viewed or imported in Scidb.
  </p>

  <p>
  Mark Crowther's <a target="_blank" href="http://www.theweekinchess.com">The Week in Chess</a>
  is providing daily chess news and games. Furthermore this site provides
  chess archives with high quality:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li><a target="_blank" href="http://www.chess.co.uk/twic/twicp1.html">TWIC 211-400</a>: 11/23/1998 to 7/8/2002</li>
    <li><a target="_blank" href="http://www.chess.co.uk/twic/twicp.html">TWIC 400...</a>: 7/8/2002 until today</li>
  </ul>

  <p>
  The site <a target="_blank" href="http://www.nowinchess.com/games.php">Now In Chess</a> by
  Gerardo Fernandez is tracking games of high quality not contained in TWIC
  archives.
  </p>

  <div class="header">
  Chess engines
  </div>

  <p>
  Many chess engines are available. A good listing is provided by
  <a target="_blank" href="http://www.computerchess.org.uk/ccrl/4040">CCRL 40/40</a>,
  which allows an overview over the strength of the engines.
  </p>

  <p>
  Of particular significance are:
  </p>

  <dir>
    <li>
    <a target="_blank" href="http://stockfishchess.org">Stockfish</a>:
    This engine is pre-installed in Scidb. Not recommended is the original
    version, because of significant programming errors (crashes). Scidb's version
    is providing an unique feature, the support of variant
    <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-check_chess">Three-Check Chess</a>.
    </li>

    <li>
    <a target="_blank" href="http://www.vlasak.biz/critter">Critter</a>:
    Also a really strong engine, unfortunately not open source.
    </li>

    <li>
    <a target="_blank" href="http://sjeng.org/indexold.html">Sjeng</a>:
    This engine - also pre-installed in Scidb - is supporting the chess variants
    <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crazyhouse">Crazyhouse</a> and
    <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antichess">Antichess</a>
    (<a target="_blank" href="http://suicidechess.ca/index.php?lg=en">Suicide</a>,
    <a target="_blank" href="http://www6.chessclub.com/help/Giveaway">Giveaway</a>,
    <a target="_blank" href="http://loserschess.org/">Losers</a>).
    </li>
  </dir>

  <div class="header">
  Other chess databases
  </div>

  <p>
  <a target="_blank" href="http://scid.sourceforge.net">Scid</a> is probably the best known free
  chess database, originally developed by Shane Hudson. The today's version
  of Scid is rather buggy.
  </p>

  <p>
  <a target="_blank" href="scidvspc.sourceforge.net">Scid vs. PC</a>, developed by
  Steven Atkinson, is a bug-fixed version of Scid, with focus on
  greater usability.
  </p>

  <p>
  Gerhard Kalab's
  <a target="_blank" href="http://code.google.com/p/scidonthego/">Scid on the Go</a>
  lets you use Scid for an Android tablet.
  </p>

  <p>
  <a target="_blank" href="http://chessx.sourceforge.net">ChessX</a>, currently developed
  by Jens Nissen, provides a free chess database specialized on the
  <a target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portable_Game_Notation">PGN Format</a>.
  </p>

  <p>
  No longer in development, but still of interest, is the Java application
  <a target="_blank" href="http://jose-chess.sourceforge.net">José</a>.
  </p>
